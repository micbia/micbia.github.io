About Me

I am an astrophysicist and cosmologist, currently PostDoc researcher in the Cosmology grop at ETH Zurich, part of the Radio Astronomy and Cosmology group of Prof. Alexandre Refregier.

My main focus is the study of Epoch of Reionization (EoR) and Post-Reionization (Post-EoR). With the help of Radiative Transfer codes, combined to hydrodynamical N-body simulations, I model the cosmological 21-cm signal expected during and after reionization .

My domain of research also include the application of Artificial Inteligence (AI) techniques to 21-cm radio observations for the mitigation of foreground contamination in inteferometry telescopes such as the Low-Frequency Array (LOFAR) and the Square Kilometre Array (SKA).

I have been part of the SKA Swiss Consortium (SKACH), since Switzerland joined the SKAO in January 2022.

Experience


PostDoc in Astrophysics
2021 - 2024

Ecole Polytecnique de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland



Developed more realistic inteferometry radio telescope observation simulations and extended the SERENEt network to mitigate foreground contaminations in 21-cm tomographic observations. Developed a flexible and GPU-accelerated RT algorithm and a Physical Informed Neural Network (PINN) to speed-up EoR simulations


Doctorate in Astrophysics
2017 - 2021

University of Sussex, United Kingdom



Implemented a stochastic, density dependent clumping model to account for sub-grid inhomogeneity in RT and N-body simulations (e.g.: RAMSES-RT, C2-Ray and CubeP3M) to simulate the observational signatures of the Epoch of Reionization. Moreover, I developed a Deep Learning segmenatation approach for the detection of neutral hydrogen in 21-cm tomographic images of SKA-Low (Link to my thesis).
